# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are members of a design team who collect and explain visual references, articles, tools, images, videos, and links throughout the week. External visitors use the same surface to observe the document as it evolves. The primary job is to contribute or read useful design discoveries without learning Markdown, switching to a separate publishing tool, or waiting for an editorial workflow.

## Product Purpose

Design Weekly turns one shared weekly document into a public, living design archive. Anyone with the link can edit the current issue anonymously, while completed weeks become read-only archive entries presented through a branded gallery. Success means collaboration feels as immediate as DingTalk Docs, the public presentation feels intentional enough for a design team, and concurrent edits never overwrite the document.

## Brand Personality

Curious, collective, and precise. The product should feel like a working design studio wall that has been organized with editorial discipline: active without being noisy, expressive without becoming decorative, and confident without becoming corporate.

## Anti-references

- A generic SaaS dashboard made from nested cards, soft shadows, and oversized rounded corners.
- A plain DingTalk or Notion clone with no independent identity.
- A decorative magazine layout that sacrifices editing clarity for serif display typography.
- A social feed with likes, comments, ranking, approval, or personal-profile mechanics.
- A glassmorphic dark interface that reduces text contrast or makes collaboration states ambiguous.

## Design Principles

1. The document is the product: editing and reading share one live surface.
2. Show the work: media, links, and contributors should be visible without extra navigation.
3. Make collaboration legible: saving, offline state, remote cursors, and archival transitions are never hidden.
4. Preserve weekly rhythm: the current issue feels alive; archived issues feel stable and browsable.
5. Keep contribution friction near zero: paste, type, upload, and continue.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast for all body and large text, preserve full keyboard editing, provide clear focus states, respect reduced-motion preferences, never rely on color alone for collaboration status, and keep mobile reading plus basic editing fully usable.
